~ Ebola Virus Disease ~

Origin and Spread

This problematic virus' first appearance was 1976 when two simultaneous outbreaks occurred. One in South Sudan, the other in the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The outbreak in the Democratic Republic of the Congo happened near the Ebola River, which is where the illness got it's name. It is believed that Ebola was introduced by fruit bats, but there is no concrete evidence. There are many ways to spread the disease, that's how so many people got it. It could be spread through touching infected animals (when preparing, cooking, or eating them); when touching body fluids of infected people (like saliva, urine, faeces, or semen); when touching things that would have the body fluids of an infected person (clothes or sheets); or when Ebola enters through cuts in the skin; or when touching someone's eyes, nose, or mouth. However, it could not be spread through the air, water, or food. 80-90% of people who were infected died, so more than 11,000 people died!

Causes and Symptoms

There are two types of symptoms, dry and wet.

Dry symptoms come first and include:

  • Fever
  • Weakness
  • Pains
  • Head aches
  • Sore throat
  • Fatigue
  • Memory loss
  • Trouble sleeping
  • Hair loss
  • Vision problems
  • Weight gain
  • Hearing loss
  • Depression
  • And anxiety

Wet symptoms come after and include:

  • Diarrhoea
  • Vomiting
  • Loss of appetite
  • Unexplained bleeding
  • Nausea
  • And abdominal pain

Other symptoms include:

  • Chest pain
  • Shortness of breath
  • Confusion
  • Red eyes
  • Skin rash
  • Hiccups
  • And seizures

People's Response

Since so many people died, the health care system knew they needed to improve their service, so they emplpoyed just under 1,500 new staff for Centers for Disease Control and Preventaion in Africa.

Even after recovering, some people were still suffering from sympyoms 2 year later, such as, feeling tired, headache, muscle and joint pains, eye pain and vision problems, weight gain, belly pain and loss of appetite, hair loss and skin problems, trouble sleeping, memory loss, hearing loss, depression and anxiety.

Many people who had "died" from Ebola virus disease, while they were being buried, they moved, an obvious sign they were alive. Although they looked like they would die again within a few hours and some people only awoke for a second, then died again. This is because their body would go into such a deep coma, that they could be declared "dead", then when they were being buried, their body would have sudden jolt of energy, awake form their coma, and some survived and were rushed to hospital, for others, the jolt of energy was too much for their bodies, and they died with seconds of awaking.
