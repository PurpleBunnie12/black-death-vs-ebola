~ The Black Death ~

Origin and Spread

The origin of the deathly plague, that took the lives of many, was in China in the early to mid 1300s. It started with a bacterium called Yersinia pestis which then infected fleas that lived on rats, leading to the many thousands of people being infected and dying. These infected rats mostly lived on trade boats. This was how the plague spread so quickly.

In October 1347 the plague hit Europe, after 12 trade ships made their way across the Black Sea and reached a port in Sicily. In 5 years, more than 20 million people died!

Causes and Symptoms

The cause of the Black Death was Yersinia pestis. This bacterium was spread through the air, through the bite of fleas and rats, and touch. People could go to sleep healthy, and be dead by morning.

There were many symptoms including:

  • Strange swellings
  • Boils with pus and blood seeping out
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhoea
  • Aches and pains
  • Then death

Recent research shows that the organism that causes the Black Death, Yersinia pestis, may have existed in Europe as early as 3000BC!

People's Response

People believed the Black Death was God's punishment because they didn't understand the biology of the plague. Therefore, many people tried to win God's forgiveness, to aviod getting the plague. Many Jews were murdered between 1348-1349, due to this misunderstanding. It was a worrying time for all. To cope with the stress, some people lashed out at their neighbours. While others were anxious about the condition of their soul and children, so they locked themselves inside as a result.
